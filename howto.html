First up, we'll start with the very basics such as tying a slip knot and your first cast on rows.

The Slip Knot

Casting on with the Long Tail Cast on Technique

Wool and the Gang

Regularly used cast on for anything and everything, especially if you need it to be stretchy!

Casting on with the Thumb cast on Technique

Sheep and Stitch

This is actually a more basic cast on technique compared to the long-tail, however it has its limitations such as stretchiness of seams.
It is a fast cast-on, but be mindful that it can cause tension issues.

The size of gauge is a very important part of knitting.

Intermediate and Advanced Tutorials

Once you've mastered all of the above techniques, it's time to move onto some more essential techniques such as increasing stitches in your work, changing colours and carrying yarn through your work.

Picking up Stitches

Wool and The Gang

Picking up stitches on the sides of a garment or ones that have been dropped are absolutely crucial to knitting and this video will show you how it works.

Difficulty: Intermediate

Sheep and Stitch

A technique that will be needed for increasing the size of your project as you work.
There are differences in how the stitch will look depending on if it is made left or right, so be sure to pay close attention to the video.

Carrying Yarn

LoveCrafts

A very useful technique when making striped garments as it means you won't have to cut the yarn and weave it in every single time you want to switch colours.

Difficulty: Advanced

Fair Isle Knitting

LoveCrafts

Fair Isle knitting is a technique commonly used with Winter style clothing, from gloves, scarves, sweaters, the works really!
However it is fairly difficult, but once mastered, is a wonder for adding distinct patterning to your items.
